Common AirPods Microphone Issues & Solutions

One Microphone Not Working

AirPods have microphones in both earbuds. If one side isn't working, try putting that AirPod back in the case and taking it out again.

Microphone Too Quiet

Check if your AirPods are clean. Debris can block the microphone openings. Gently clean with a soft, dry cloth or a small brush.

Cutting Out During Calls

This could be a Bluetooth interference issue. Try moving away from other electronic devices or Wi-Fi routers.

Too Much Background Noise

AirPods Pro and Max have noise cancellation for audio in, but all models use beam-forming mics. Stay in quieter environments for best results.

AirPods Microphone Tips

  • Battery Level Matters: Low battery can affect microphone performance. Make sure your AirPods are charged.
  • iOS Settings: On iPhone, go to Settings > Bluetooth > [i] next to AirPods to access additional microphone settings.
  • Firmware Updates: Make sure your AirPods have the latest firmware by keeping them connected to your iOS device regularly.
  • Reset if Necessary: If problems persist, try resetting your AirPods by pressing and holding the button on the back of the case.
